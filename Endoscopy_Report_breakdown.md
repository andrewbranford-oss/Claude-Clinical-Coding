# Endoscopy Report Breakdown

## Patient Data Input
Patient and episode data may be supplied as an approved `.xlsm` workbook for batch coding review. Treat the workbook as patient-identifiable clinical data, do not execute embedded macros, and retain workbook name, worksheet, row number, and relevant column headings as source evidence for each extracted field.

When codes are proposed from the endoscopy report or workbook data, verify ICD-10 and OPCS-4 candidates against the NHS England Classifications Browser at https://classbrowser.nhs.uk/ using the version appropriate to the episode date.

---

## Indication for Exam
**ICD-10 diagnosis coding** – this section will tell us the reason patient is having the endoscopy. If no diagnosis is made from the findings, the indication(s) will be the first codes assigned.

---

## Procedure Performed
**OPCS coding** – this will be the general description for the procedure performed.

---

## Medications
> ⚠️ **We do not use this section.**

---

## Extent of Exam
**OPCS coding** – this will tell us the furthest point that was reached by the endoscope.

---

## Findings
**ICD-10 diagnosis coding and OPCS procedure coding** – this section tells us what diagnoses/conditions were found during the endoscopy and also what interventions were performed e.g. biopsy/resection/polypectomy etc.

---

## NED Procedure Diagnosis
**ICD-10 coding** – this can describe the diagnosis made from the endoscopy or any findings.

---

## NED Biopsy / Pathology
**OPCS coding** – this section details any biopsies taken or excised lesions sent for examination.

---

## Recommendation
We wouldn't typically use this section but it can sometimes provide clarification on what diagnoses are still being investigated.

---

## Limitations of Exam
> Not typically used by coding.

---

## Complications
**ICD-10 and OPCS coding** – details any complications and any procedures needed to treat them.
